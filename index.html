About Me

Hello, my name is Jaysson Diaz and I am a devoted student programmer who is currently attending New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT) and majoring in Computer Science. I have had a deep passion for developing computer programs since I was 10 years old but my interest peaked when I took a college level computer science course. Althought I have only been in college for half a year, I have made strides in getting involved on campus and developing myself professionally and personally.

I am a national member of the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers (SHPE), which I have been really involved with because of how friendly all of the members are. SHPE is an excellent place for connections with fellow future engineers and potential employers. I am a member of SHPE's Webmaster Committee where I am learning more about HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Bootstrap in order to build a new NJIT SHPE website.

I am also a national member of the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM). This club is more focused toward my interests as a computer science major. The club hosts events such as workshops on command line, git and vim that proved to be extremely informative. There are also special interest groups(SIG) that gives students knowledge beyond what is taught in class. I am a member of SIG Web where I have been learning a good amount about back and front end web development.

A project that I am especially fond of is a hackathon project called TranSend that my teammates and I made in 25 hours. TranSend is a chat application that translates incoming text into another language. TranSend uses socket.io, node.js, Google Translate API, and other web development tools. TranSend won the 'Best Use of AWS' catagory at HackNJIT.

I am currently the webmaster for NJIT's ACM chapter, where I am working on improving the hackNJIT and programming club websites by using PHP, Javascript, Node.js, and other web technologies.

TranSend
Our HackNJIT submission that comprised of a web chat application that implemented socket.io, node.js, and Amazon Web Services. Yet, our team wants to improve our app by applying some of these features: Supporting more languages, reversing to original text, feedback from bilingual users, additional graphics/improved presentation, mobile, login system/expanded security, integration with Wolfram Alpha for automatic currency and measurement conversions
